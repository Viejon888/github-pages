Academic Architecture

Curriculum

Pillar 1

Liberal Arts Core

20+ units

Calculus I or higher, writing, and humanities. At least 20 of 128 total units must come from non-business areas — ensuring every Stern graduate has genuine breadth.

Pillar 2

Business Core

30 units

Business Tools (~14u): Microeconomics, Statistics & Regression, Financial Accounting, Cohort Leadership (P/F).

Functional Core (~16u): Choose 4 of 6 — Managerial Accounting, Finance, IT & Society, Mgmt & Org, Marketing, Operations.

Pillar 3 — Unique to Stern

Social Impact Core

4 courses over 4 years

The first mandatory ethics and social impact sequence at any undergraduate business school.

Y1: Business & Society · Y2: Org. Communication · Y3: Law, Business & Society · Y4: Professional Responsibility & Leadership.

Two courses cited as models by the Carnegie Foundation.

Pillar 4

Business Concentration

12 units · Required

Declare at least 1 of 12 concentrations by end of junior year. A second co-concentration is permitted. All students leave with a clearly defined academic specialty.

Pillar 5

Global Business Requirement

4 units + Experience

All students complete Economics of Global Business (EGB). Starting with Class of 2027, a full Global Experience is mandatory — study away at one of 16 NYU global sites or 17 IBEX partner schools.

Pillar 6 — Unique to Stern

NYC Consulting Capstone

3–4 units · Required Senior

Introduced 2023–24; 1,000+ completions already. Faculty-led teams spend a full semester solving real challenges for corporate partners including Microsoft and TIFIN — structured as an actual consulting engagement.

44 elective units remain for minors, second major, or study away.

Electives

44 units available for a minor, second NYU major (via College of Arts & Science), second Stern concentration, or global study. Access to 100+ minors across Tisch, Wagner, Steinhardt, and other NYU schools.

Layer 1

General Education

USC-wide requirement

WRIT 150 & WRIT 340, MATH 118gx or higher, two semesters of economics (ECON 351x and 352x). All courses taken for a letter grade — no CR/NC electives in core.

Layer 2

Business Core

Three-tiered architecture

Foundational: Math, statistics, accounting, economics, communication.

Functional: Finance, Marketing, Organizational Behavior, Operations.

Integrative: Strategy plus a new 2-unit data modeling class introduced to strengthen analytics fluency.

Layer 3 — Unique to Marshall

Experiential Learning Center

Embedded throughout

The first behavioral lab program for undergrad business in the nation — not a standalone capstone but woven into all four years. Students practice group decision-making, negotiations, presentations, and interviews with immediate recorded feedback throughout multiple courses.

Layer 4

Upper-Division Electives

12 units · 3 courses

300- or 400-level courses from ACCT, BAEP, BUCO, DSO, FBE, MKT, MOR, or RISK. Can also be fulfilled via a Marshall-sponsored international exchange semester abroad.

Layer 5

Optional Emphasis

Optional — not required

Unlike Stern's mandatory concentration, Marshall emphases are entirely optional. Students graduate with a general BSBA and may layer an emphasis for focused depth — or keep the degree generalist and exploit the free elective space instead.

Layer 6 — Unique to Marshall

Free Electives

32 units

The most generous free elective block of the two programs — enough for a full minor, a double major, or deep language acquisition. Designed explicitly to leverage USC's multi-school ecosystem: Viterbi, Cinematic Arts, Price, Iovine & Young.

Key Distinction

Marshall's "breadth with depth" philosophy maximizes flexibility — no forced specialization — while Stern's pillar system guarantees every graduate has covered ethics, global business, functional business, and a real corporate consulting engagement before graduating.

International Exposure

Global Programs

NYU Stern — Global Required (Class of 2027+)

NYU Study Away (16 Sites)

Spend at least one term at any of NYU's 16 global study sites worldwide — Abu Dhabi, Shanghai, London, Paris, Madrid, Berlin, Florence, Prague, Buenos Aires, Accra, Sydney, and more. The most expansive network of any school on this list.

IBEX Exchange Program

A semester at one of 17 partner business schools in Europe and Asia. Available to juniors and seniors. Schools include top institutions across the UK, France, Germany, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Japan.

Stern Around the World (SAW)

Short-term immersion courses pairing semester coursework with travel to Mexico, Japan, Singapore, Costa Rica, and Hawaii. Structured around real business environments in each destination.

BPE — London & Shanghai (Built-In)

Business & Political Economy students spend sophomore fall in London and junior spring in Shanghai — two mandatory abroad semesters built directly into the degree structure.

NYU describes itself as "the world's first truly global university" — with campuses across six continents, this isn't marketing language.

USC Marshall — Global Optional (Rich Portfolio)

Global Leadership Program (GLP)

By invitation for top incoming students. A year-long leadership seminar plus a spring break immersive trip to Thailand — building a cohort of Marshall's highest-potential undergrads from day one.

LINC — First-Year for All Students

Open to all first-year Marshall students. A 2-unit course paired with an 8–10 day immersive trip to Dubai, Singapore, Seoul, Tokyo, or Madrid. Rare for a first-year program to offer this level of international access.

Winslow-Maxwell Global Internships

8–10 week overseas internships in London, Madrid, Berlin, Rome, Prague, Sydney, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Tokyo. One of the most geographically diverse internship programs in undergraduate business.

World Bachelor in Business (WBB)

The only program of its kind: a 4-year degree spanning USC (Los Angeles), HKUST (Hong Kong), and Bocconi (Milan). Full cultural immersion and academic collaboration across three continents. Accepted students are admitted directly to all three institutions.

MISIP — Indonesia & Thailand

8–10 week internships in Southeast Asia led by prominent USC alumni, offering emerging market exposure unlike any program at Stern.

Marshall's WBB is widely regarded as the most globally immersive undergraduate business degree offered by any American university.

NYU Stern

Choose Stern to be at the center of finance.

  • Finance or IB are primary goals, and NYC internships during the year are a structural advantage you want to use
  • A mandatory, four-year ethics and social impact curriculum resonates with how you want to be trained
  • You want access to NYU's 16 global study sites or the BPE's built-in London and Shanghai semesters
  • The BTE program (business + computer science + entrepreneurship) matches your interdisciplinary interests
  • You want the integrated BS/MS Accounting dual degree for CPA licensure in an accelerated track

USC Marshall

Choose Marshall to have LA's ecosystem behind you.

  • Entertainment, tech, real estate, or consulting on the West Coast is the target — Marshall's network is unmatched there
  • Interdisciplinary joint degrees with engineering, cinematic arts, AI, or public policy genuinely appeal to you
  • The Trojan network's intensity — they hire each other, aggressively — is a compelling multi-decade differentiator
  • You want 32 free elective units and the flexibility of optional (not mandatory) specialization
  • The World Bachelor in Business (LA, Hong Kong, Milan) is a once-in-a-generation academic opportunity you can't find anywhere else
