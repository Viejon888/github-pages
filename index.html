Across the Atlantic.

Italian and Spanish institutions operate under different reporting regimes. Bocconi and IE publish formula-driven, transparent admission rules — but no acceptance rates. The inverse of US schools, which publish the funnel but obscure the formula.

Patterns
worth noting.

Seven cross-cutting observations from reading every school's CDS line by line. The most selective institutions tend to publish the least.

01

Admit rate suppression at the top

Stanford doesn't print its admit rate or yield. Northeastern reports only its Boston subset. Columbia withholds all GPA and waitlist data. The most selective schools publish the least.

02

Race / ethnicity, three ways post-SFFA

Three approaches: (a) row deleted from the grid (Stanford), (b) row present but no column checked (NYU, UT), (c) row marked Not Considered (UCLA, pre-existing under Prop 209).

03

GPA disclosure is the most uneven field

Tufts, BC, Columbia, Georgetown, Northeastern, UT Austin all leave GPA distributions blank. NYU, BU, Stanford, UCLA, USC, UPenn report them. No common standard.

04

Demonstrated interest splits the field

"Considered" or higher at NYU, BU, Northeastern. Explicitly Not Considered at Columbia, Stanford, Tufts, Georgetown, UPenn, USC, UCLA, BC. A signal of how schools manage yield.

05

Legacy still alive at most privates

"Considered" at UPenn, USC, BC, Tufts, Stanford, Columbia, BU. Not Considered at NYU, UCLA, Northeastern, Georgetown. UT does not consider it (state law).

06

Test-required is now rare

In this group, only UT Austin and Georgetown required tests for the 2024–25 reported cycle. UPenn and Stanford announced a return to required for Fall 2026.

07

Europe inverts the disclosure model

Bocconi publishes the exact admission formula (55/45 split). IE publishes its five-stage process and credential thresholds. Neither publishes acceptance rates. US schools publish the funnel and obscure the formula — Europe publishes the formula and obscures the funnel.
